Conversation

Before the dentist enters:

  • Review changes in medical history, allergies, and medications
    • Flag any bleeding disorders, heart conditions, and medications
  • Confirm tooth number for extraction
  • Check in out patient's emotional state
    • Look for visible signs of stress or anxiety, so take a moment to ask what is on their mind and address any worries to the best of your ability
  • Walk patient through the procedure (espeically if first extraction)
    • Cracking noises are normal
      • Reassure the patient that the tooth sometimes needs to be sectioned or may break on its own if it is weakened, this is expected and nothing to worry about
    • Tell patient to raise their hand if they feel any pain or need a break
      • We will stop immediately and offer a break or additional anesthetic, reassure the patient that they are always in control

After treatment:

  • Go over surgical post-op instructions
    • Bite on gauze for 60 minutes with gentle pressure
    • Ice pack: 20 minutets on / 20 minutes off for first 24 hours
      • "Ice cream from the inside, ice pack from the outside" - Dr. Shirbani
    • Rest for the remainder of the day; keep head elevated when sleeping
    • No exercise or activity that raises heart rate for 48 hours
    • No smoking, vaping, or alcohol for 48 hours
    • Soft foods only for the first 2-3 days
      • Nothing too hot as heat can increase blood flow towards and cause bleedings
      • Avoid hard, crunchy, or spicy foods
      • Avoid foods with small seeds or fine seasonings
      • No straws (no sucking or spitting motion)
    • Brush teeth gently but completely avoid extraction site
      • If patient is hesitant, let them know they can skip brushing their teeth tonight
    • After 24 hours: rinse gently with warm salt water (2-3x/day)
      • Do NOT spit forcefully
      • Do NOT rinse vigorously
    • Do NOT touch or disturb the extraction site
      • If sutures are used, they are resorbable and will dissolve on their own within about a week
    • Review any prescribed medications and confirm instructions
  • Some pain and discomfort is normal after extraction
    • If abnormal amount of pain persists for longer than 2-3 weeks, tell patient to make an appointment with us right away
  • Emphasize to the patient that in case of servere, non-stop bleeding, they should go to the emergency room right away
